Retired Nurses

According to Dr. Peter Buerhaus’s 2017 report in the Journal of Nursing regulation, he and his colleagues predict 100 million RN’S will retire. This will leave a huge void in patient care settings & other organizations that depend on the knowledge and presence of experienced RN’S.

75,000 students
turned away

According to AACN’s report on 2018-2019 Enrollment and Graduations in Baccalaureate Programs in Nursing, U.S nursing schools turned away more than 75,000 qualified applicants from baccalaureate and graduate nursing programs in 2018 due to insufficient number of faculty, clinical sites, classroom space, and clinical preceptors, as well as budget constraints.

80%
baccalaureate

In October of 2010, the Institute of Medicine released its landmark report on “The Future Nursing,” initiated by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, which called for increasing the number of baccalaureate-prepared nurses in the workforce to 80% and doubling the amount of nurses with doctoral degrees. The current nursing workforce falls far short of these recommendations with only 56% RN’s prepared at the baccalaureate or graduate level.
